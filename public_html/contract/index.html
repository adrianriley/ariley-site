I am a freelance software developer resident in Sheffield, South Yorkshire, U. K. I have a Ph.D. and a B.Sc. in Pure Mathematics, both from the University of Sheffield. I have over 30 years experience in the I.T. industry as developer, team leader, project manager, adviser and trainer.

Over the past 10 years, I have been working mostly in Java. I have development experience with many Java technologies, e.g. J2EE, EJB, servlets, JSP, JSF, Spring, Struts, JFC/Swing, AWT, applets, JAXP, JavaMail, JMS, and also in related areas such as XML, HTML, XSLT, Web services, Web servers and servlet engines (Apache, Tomcat, WebSphere, NES, JRun). I have experience of using many popular RDBMSs, particularly Oracle, Postgres and DB2. I also have extensive experience in C, C++, and Fortran, and knowledge of several other languages, such as Pascal, Algol68, Algol, Cobol and various assembly languages.

I have used several varieties of Windows, from 3.1 to 10, and many flavours of Unix, e.g. Solaris, AIX, HP/UX, DG/UX as well as several Linux variants. I also have experience of other operating systems, many now half-forgotten cyber dinosaurs.

I have worked in diverse industries; finance, education, engineering, health, TV, architecture, library and information systems, etc., in the UK, the Netherlands and the USA. Recent clients include BJSS, KIT digital (now known as Piksel), Technophobia (now known as G2G3 Digital, South Yorkshire Police, IBM (for IBM clients Camelot, CIS and Bradford and Bingley plc), Capita, HSBC, Standard Life, Mass Consultants, Synectic Systems, BancTec, ESIS (now part of Logica), Fretwell Downing Informatics.

I speak some French and a little German.
